What is a Merchant Cash Advance?

Merchant cash advances (MCAs) can be a fast, flexible way to access capital—when you understand how they work. This guide breaks down key terms, timelines, and tips to stay in control.

By Jordan Reyes, Founder & CEO, RedHat Funding

Share:

Merchant cash advance basics

An MCA provides a lump sum of working capital in exchange for a portion of your future sales. Instead of fixed monthly payments, you remit a percentage of your card sales or bank deposits until the agreed-upon payback amount is reached. This percentage is called the holdback, while the total amount you repay is governed by a factor rate.

Because repayment adjusts with your revenue, MCAs can be a good fit for businesses with seasonal fluctuations or growth opportunities that need fast funding.

How to prepare your application

Gather these documents before you speak with your funding manager:

  • Recent three months of business bank statements
  • Voided business check or official bank letter
  • Government-issued ID for the owner
  • Basic business information (legal name, EIN, time in business, average monthly revenue)

Upload files through our encrypted portal for faster underwriting. Accuracy matters—double-check that statement pages are complete and legible.

Model cash flow before you sign

Holdback percentages typically range from 8% to 20% of your daily deposits. Use recent sales data to forecast how the holdback will impact your cash on hand. We build side-by-side scenarios so you know the projected payoff date and any early payoff discounts.

If your business has multiple revenue streams, consider how each will contribute to repayment. For example, restaurants may have card and delivery sales, while contractors may depend on ACH deposits from clients.

Avoid predatory pitfalls

Not all MCA contracts are created equal. Watch out for confession of judgment clauses, double-funded offers, or requirements to open new bank accounts. RedHat Funding vets every partner and negotiates on your behalf to remove risky language.

Ask us to review any contract you receive—even from other brokers. We’ll flag hidden fees and ensure you understand total payback.

Put your advance to work

Business owners use MCA funding for inventory, payroll, marketing campaigns, equipment repairs, or new locations. Tie your advance to a clear ROI plan with milestones so you can evaluate success and schedule check-ins with your funding manager.

Plan your renewal strategy

As you repay your balance, we’ll monitor opportunities to renew or refinance into better terms. Many providers offer additional capital once 50–60% of the balance is repaid. Keep us updated on revenue trends so we can time renewal offers to your goals.
